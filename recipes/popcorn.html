William's Popcorn Recipe

Learning how to make popcorn is fun and useful because it is a quick, tasty snack perfect for movie nights or sharing with friends.

How to Make Popcorn:

  1. Place a large pot with a tight-fitting lid on the stove and add the oil. Heat on medium.
  2. Add three popcorn kernels to the pot and cover with the lid.
  3. When those test kernels pop, add the rest of the 1/3 cup kernels in an even layer. Cover again.
  4. Shake the pot gently so kernels stay in an even layer and don't burn. Keep the heat at medium.
  5. As the kernels begin to pop, gently lift and shake the pot occasionally so unpopped kernels fall to the bottom.
  6. When popping slows to about 2 to 3 seconds between pops, remove the pot from heat and keep the lid on for a minute to let remaining pops finish.
  7. Carefully remove the lid (watch for steam), pour popcorn into a large bowl, and season with salt, melted butter, or other toppings.

Safety Tips When Making Popcorn:

  1. Always use a pot with a tight-fitting lid to prevent hot oil from splattering.
  2. Do not overheat the oil — keep the heat on medium to avoid burning.
  3. Wear oven mitts when handling the pot, as it can get very hot.
  4. Be careful when lifting the lid — hot steam can cause burns.
  5. Never leave popcorn unattended while it is popping, as it can burn quickly.
  6. Keep children away from the stove or microwave while making popcorn.
  7. Allow the popcorn to cool slightly before eating to avoid burning your mouth.

Enjoy your snack
